# Security Decisions Under Pressure Are Different Decisions

Deadlines drive projects. They also change how we decide what is secure.

Under pressure, thoroughness takes a backseat. Quick fixes become acceptable solutions. This shift can leave gaps. Attackers thrive on these compromises.

---

## Pressure Warps Perception of Risk

When deadlines loom, risk assessments change. What was once unacceptable becomes tolerated. A temporary permission elevation for a developer or an overlooked vulnerability in a third-party library can seem inconsequential. These decisions are not inherently bad. They just do not account for the bigger picture.

*Pressure narrows focus.*

---

## Shortcuts Create Vulnerabilities

Cutting corners to meet deadlines often means skipping steps in security protocols. This could be anything from bypassing code reviews to ignoring software updates. Each shortcut introduces a potential weakness. These vulnerabilities are not isolated issues. They create entry points that can align with other oversights, forming pathways for attacks.

*Attackers do not need much to start.*

---

## Stress Leads to Overlooked Details

High-pressure environments make it easy to miss details. A misconfigured firewall rule or an unsecured API endpoint might go unnoticed. These small errors can have large consequences. They provide opportunities for attackers to gain access, escalate privileges, and move laterally within your systems. The longer these oversights persist, the greater the risk.

*Every detail matters.*

---

## Final Thought

Pressure changes how we make decisions. It forces us to compromise on security standards that would otherwise be non-negotiable. These compromises create vulnerabilities. And attackers are always ready to exploit them.
